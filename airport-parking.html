Head to Head

Park & Ride vs Meet & Greet

Not sure which parking type is right for your trip? Here is a detailed breakdown to help you decide.

Park & Ride

Budget-friendly off-airport parking

Park & Ride is the most popular choice for families and frequent travellers looking to save money without compromising on security or convenience. You drive to a managed, CCTV-monitored car park close to the airport and then board a frequent shuttle bus that takes you directly to your terminal.

Shuttle buses typically run every 10–15 minutes and the transfer rarely takes more than 20 minutes. Most Park & Ride car parks are fully enclosed or have ANPR camera systems, and many offer covered parking to protect your vehicle from the elements.

This is the ideal option for longer holidays where savings can be significant — you could save up to 70% compared to official on-airport car parks. Book well in advance for the very best prices.

Meet & Greet

Premium valet parking at the terminal

Meet & Greet is the ultimate in stress-free airport parking. You drive directly to your departure terminal and a professional, uniformed chauffeur meets you kerbside. You hand over your keys and walk straight into check-in — no shuttle bus, no long walks, no hassle.

When your flight lands, your car will be returned to the arrivals area. This service is particularly popular with business travellers, families with young children or lots of luggage, and anyone catching a very early morning flight who does not want to navigate an unfamiliar car park in the dark.

While it costs more than Park & Ride, it can still represent excellent value compared to a taxi — especially for longer trips. Many customers find the time saved and convenience worth every penny.

Help & Advice

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about booking airport parking with Holiday Extras.

How far in advance should I book airport parking?

The earlier you book, the better the price — this is true for almost every type of airport parking. Prices can increase significantly in the weeks before your travel date as availability fills up. As a general rule, booking more than 6 weeks in advance gives you access to the lowest prices. Many travellers book their parking at the same time as their flights to lock in the best rates. Even booking 2–3 weeks ahead can save you considerably compared to booking on the day.

Can I cancel or amend my airport parking booking?

Most airport parking bookings made through Holiday Extras include free cancellation, provided you cancel before the parking start date. This makes it risk-free to book early and lock in low prices. You can also amend your booking dates or times online via your Holiday Extras account or by contacting our customer services team. Some promotional rates may be non-refundable, which will be clearly stated at the time of booking.

What is the difference between Park and Ride and Meet and Greet?

With Park and Ride, you drive to an off-airport car park, leave your vehicle in a secure facility, and then take a shuttle bus to the terminal. This is the most economical option, suited to families or anyone on a tighter budget. The shuttle service runs frequently and the total time from parking to terminal is usually 20–30 minutes.

Meet and Greet is a premium valet service where you drive directly to your terminal, hand your keys to a professional chauffeur, and walk straight into the airport. On return, your car is brought to the arrivals area for you. There is no shuttle bus and no walk — just complete convenience from door to door.

Is airport parking safe and secure?

Yes. Every car park we list on Holiday Extras is individually vetted and must meet our minimum security requirements before we will feature it. The majority of our car parks carry the Park Mark Safer Parking Award, a nationally recognised accreditation scheme run in conjunction with the British Police. Security features typically include CCTV, perimeter fencing, barrier-controlled access, security lighting, and regular patrols. For Meet and Greet operators, we require full public liability insurance and a bond to ensure your vehicle is protected.

How do I get from the car park to my terminal?

This depends on the type of parking you book. For Park and Ride, a courtesy shuttle bus will take you directly to and from your terminal. Buses typically run every 10–15 minutes and the journey takes between 5 and 25 minutes depending on the car park's distance from the airport. Your confirmation email will include full instructions on where to find the shuttle pick-up point. For Park and Walk, you simply stroll to the terminal. For Meet and Greet, there is no transfer needed — you are dropped at the terminal door.

What happens if my flight is delayed or my return changes?

Most car parks will accommodate late returns at no extra charge if your flight is delayed, particularly if you contact them or Holiday Extras as soon as possible. For Park and Ride car parks, shuttle buses continue to operate and will collect you when your delayed flight lands. For Meet and Greet services, your operator will track your flight and adjust accordingly. If your return changes significantly, we recommend contacting the car park directly using the phone number in your booking confirmation. Our 24/7 customer service team is also available to help rearrange your booking.
